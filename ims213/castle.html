CHARLIE HA

Gates of the Bloodmoon Castle

Project type:

Castle Level Design in Unreal Engine.

Project description:

There are two goals with this project: First, to familiarize myself with a common practice in contemporary game design and development: "Kit-bashing." And second, to create a portfolio-worthy 30 second fly-through of a tiny medieval fantasy scene of my own creation

For this project, my instructor provided me with kit-bash ready components for a fantasy medieval castle and village. My goal is to create a little landscape, build a little castle, fill it with a few modest houses, and then create a portfolio-worthy "fly-through". It should appear as though it were a single take

I brought up the kit-bash component map to get a sense of the myriad pieces available to me. Then I went to Pinterest to research references, and whiteboxed the castle to create a cardboard vision of my castle in its proper place on the landscape.

------------------

I started the fly through at the entrance with 2 lit up giant stone lions, half of a moon is blocked by the castle entrance as an anticipation for the viewer, the fog blurs the back so only the stone lions are clear. The camera slowly approaches the 2 giant doors, then the doors open, there are stone horses on two sides and a pathway to the middle of the castle, leading to a huge tower with a goddess statue on top, and the full blood moon in the back. The camera flys up and the fog clears revealing the whole castle from the top down view.

Game Mechanics:

TNT traps that is triggered and explodes when the player walks near them. The trap has 1 second delay with sound cue to let player know and escape.

A hit indicator that flashes the screen red when the player takes damage.

Minecarts that move on rails when player jumps into the cart.

Door that requires a key for the player to open. The door also has a red light that turns green when the player has the Jerry Can.

A pressure plate where the player can pay $14 to buy a Jerry Can.

Timer that counts down from 6 minutes to 0, and when it reaches 0, the game ends.

When your health reaches 0, the game ends.

A bell that rings when time is 3 minutes left.

NPC free roam.
